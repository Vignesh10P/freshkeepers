        <div class="recipe">
            <h2>1. Paneer Butter Masala</h2>
            <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
            <div class="ingredients">
                <ul>
                    <li>200g paneer, cubed</li>
                    <li>1 large onion, finely chopped</li>
                    <li>1 tomato, pureed</li>
                    <li>2 tbsp butter</li>
                    <li>1 tsp ginger-garlic paste</li>
                    <li>1 tsp garam masala</li>
                    <li>1 tsp red chili powder</li>
                    <li>1/2 cup cream</li>
                    <li>Salt to taste</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
            <h3>Steps:</h3>
            <div class="steps">
                <ol>
                    <li>Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions until golden brown.</li>
                    <li>Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes.</li>
                    <li>Stir in pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the gravy.</li>
                    <li>Add garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.</li>
                    <li>Add the paneer cubes and cook for 5-7 minutes.</li>
                    <li>Stir in cream and cook for another 2-3 minutes.</li>
                    <li>Serve hot with naan or rice.</li>
                </ol>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="recipe">
            <h2>2. Paneer Tikka</h2>
            <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
            <div class="ingredients">
                <ul>
                    <li>200g paneer, cut into cubes</li>
                    <li>2 tbsp yogurt</li>
                    <li>1 tbsp lemon juice</li>
                    <li>1 tsp turmeric powder</li>
                    <li>1 tsp red chili powder</li>
                    <li>1 tsp garam masala</li>
                    <li>1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste</li>
                    <li>Salt to taste</li>
                    <li>Oil for grilling</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
            <h3>Steps:</h3>
            <div class="steps">
                <ol>
                    <li>Mix yogurt, lemon juice, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, ginger-garlic paste, and salt in a bowl.</li>
                    <li>Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and mix well. Let it marinate for 30 minutes.</li>
                    <li>Preheat the grill or oven to medium heat and brush with some oil.</li>
                    <li>Thread the marinated paneer onto skewers and grill for 10-12 minutes until slightly charred.</li>
                    <li>Serve with mint chutney and enjoy!</li>
                </ol>
            </div>
        </div>

        <div class="recipe">
            <h2>3. Palak Paneer</h2>
            <h3>Ingredients:</h3>
            <div class="ingredients">
                <ul>
                    <li>200g paneer, cubed</li>
                    <li>1 bunch spinach (palak), chopped</li>
                    <li>1 onion, finely chopped</li>
                    <li>2 tomatoes, pureed</li>
                    <li>1 tsp cumin seeds</li>
                    <li>1 tsp turmeric powder</li>
                    <li>1 tsp garam masala</li>
                    <li>1/2 cup cream</li>
                    <li>Salt to taste</li>
                </ul>
            </div>
            <h3>Steps:</h3>
            <div class="steps">
                <ol>
                    <li>Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, then blend into a smooth paste.</li>
                    <li>Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and sauté chopped onions until golden brown.</li>
                    <li>Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates.</li>
                    <li>Stir in turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the spinach paste and cook for 5 minutes.</li>
                    <li>Add paneer cubes and cook for 5 minutes.</li>
                    <li>Stir in cream and cook for 2-3 more minutes.</li>
                    <li>Serve with roti or rice.</li>
                </ol>
            </div>
        </div>
